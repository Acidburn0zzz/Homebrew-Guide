Section 4: Using CFW

Congratulations! By this point you have:

  • Learned how to enter RCM
  • Learned how to launch fusee-gelee payloads
  • Learned how to perform NAND backups
  • Learned how to block as much Nintendo telemetry as possible

This section provides some extra info about using your newly found powers.

If something goes wrong or you need help, check troubleshooting.

Launching Atmosphere CFW

Atmosphere is currently the CFW with the largest feature set. It enables game modding, homebrew, backup installs, and more. To launch Atmosphere:

  1. Enter RCM on your Switch
  2. Push the Hekate payload to your Switch
  3. Select 'Launch > CFW'

Running other payloads within Hekate

Hekate itself supports chainloading other fusee-payloads so you can, for example, launch ReiNX from Hekate.

  1. Enter RCM on your Switch
  2. Push the Hekate payload to your Switch
  3. Select 'Launch > Payloads'
  4. Select your payload of choice

Payloads go in the '/bootloader/payloads' folder on your SD card.

Configuring Auto-Boot with Hekate

Hekate allows you to automatically launch CFW immediately after pushing a payload.

  1. Enter RCM on your Switch
  2. Push the Hekate payload to your Switch
  3. Select 'Options > Auto boot'
  4. Select your launch configuration of choice

Next time you run Hekate, instead of seeing the menu, you will be greeted with a splash screen and immediately launch into the configuration you picked.

If you need to get back into the menu, hold Volume Down when sending the Hekate payload.

Enabling AutoRCM

AutoRCM is an optional software method of forcing your Switch to go into RCM on every launch, without the need of a jig or hardmod. Essentially, you are purposefully bricking your Switch in a controlled matter that forces it to launch into recovery. This might sound scary but is not actually dangerous, and can be undone at any time.

By doing this, you will need to use a payload sender to boot your Switch after every restart/shutdown.

  1. Enter Hekate
  2. Select 'Tools > AutoRCM > Enable AutoRCM'

You can disable AutoRCM by entering the same menu above and selecting 'Disable AutoRCM'.

From now on, to boot into stock firmware, select 'Launch > Stock' in Hekate.

Accessing the Homebrew Menu

You can access the Homebrew Menu from within Atmosphere and ReiNX by loading Album from the Home Menu. To load Album normally, hold R when loading Album.

Homebrew usually comes in .nro format. Put these in the '/switch' folder on your SD card.

Installing NSP files (home menu applications)

NSP is a standard format used for digital applications.

Installing NSP files is the easiest way to get yourself banned! You shouldn't do this if you want to keep using your Switch online.

  1. Create a 'tinfoil' folder on your SD card, and a 'nsp' folder inside that
  2. Copy your NSP file to the '/tinfoil/nsp' folder you just created on your SD card.
  3. Launch into Atmosphere CFW or ReiNX
  4. Load the Homebrew Launcher (album icon)
  5. Launch Tinfoil and install your NSPs

After installing, your applications will appear on the Home Menu.

Backing Up Save Files

You can backup and restore save files locally, without Nintendo Switch Online, by using Checkpoint from within the Homebrew Menu. Dumped saves will go in '/switch/checkpoint/' on your SD card for you to copy elsewhere. These save backups can later be restored to any user account on any Switch.
